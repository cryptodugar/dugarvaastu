Origins of Vaastu Shastra

Vaastu means abode for gods and humans. Vaastu Shastra is a unique science which can be traced back to ancient India, which is used to reap the benefits of the "panch Tatvas"( five basic elements) of universe. These elements are Akash(Sky),Prithvi (Earth), jal(water), and Vaayu(wind).
“Vaastu Purusha” is the core part of the Vaastu shastra and most of the Vaastu rules are governed by his position on a grid. It’s a formless creature who blocked heaven from the earth. “Brahma” with many other gods trapped him on the ground. Hence Vastu Purusha Mandala portions were allocated hierarchically to each god based on their contributions and positions with Brahma occupying the central position.
 North- Kubera- lord of wealth (Finance)
 South- Yama- lord of death - Yama (Damaging)
 East- Indra- solar God- Aditya (Seeing the world)
 West- Varuna- lord of water (Physical)
 Northeast {Eshanya} - Ruled by Shiva
 Southeast- Agni- Fire God - Agni (Energy Generating)
 Northwest- Vayu- God of winds (Advertisement)
 Southwest- Pitru/Nairutya,Niruthi- Ruled by ancestors (History)
 Center- Brahma- Ruled by the creator of the universe (Desire)
